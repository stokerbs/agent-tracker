Tracing assets before you begin legal proceedings is essential when property or property rights have been violated — it gives your case the best chance of being accepted by the court. There are several steps worth taking to prepare an asset search before you file. Here is what we recommend to start with:

- **Trace your own assets.** Begin by searching for and gathering information about the property or property rights you want to recover. Make copies of relevant documents — contracts, registration certificates, or records of asset transfers — along with details of the damage caused by the violation.

- **Search for information and verify it.** Once you have the basics, dig deeper into the status of the assets — registration, title registration, or a direct asset search — to avoid filing a case the court won't accept. You may need to consult a legal expert to assess accuracy and estimate the value of the assets.

- **Calculate the damages.** With enough information about the assets and the violation, assess the damage done. This may include the value of lost property, damages related to the violation, or losses expected in the future. Calculating damages can be complex, so consult a lawyer for further advice.

- **Copy and preserve the evidence.** To prevent the loss or destruction of evidence connected to the case, make copies of important information and keep everything you have in order — copies of relevant documents, or records of messages relating to the violation.

- **Consult a lawyer.** Once you've gathered the information and prepared your asset search, consult a lawyer experienced in property and litigation. They can help you analyse how strong your case is, advise on how to proceed, and support you through the filing process.

Preparation before you file matters. A well-prepared asset search — with the accuracy and value of the assets properly assessed — is a key ingredient in a case that has a real chance of succeeding in court.
