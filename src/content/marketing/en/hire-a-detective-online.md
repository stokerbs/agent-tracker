## Can you really hire a detective online?

The question many people ask is, "Can you actually hire a detective online?" The answer is **yes** — but working online requires good communication and understanding between client and investigator, because most of the process starts from the information you provide: basic details about the person to be investigated, or the specifics of the case.

## What to prepare when hiring a detective online

- **Basic information** — such as the name, phone number or social-media accounts of the person to be investigated.
- **The goal of the investigation** — state your objective clearly, whether it's finding a person, an [infidelity investigation](/en/cheating-spouse-investigator), or tracing [assets](/en/asset-investigation).
- **A timeframe** — set the period in which you'd like the work completed, so both sides can plan clearly. Full communication lets the investigator work quickly and precisely. Hiring online also cuts out the hassle of meetings and document handovers, making the whole process smoother and more efficient.

## What to watch out for when hiring online

Convenient as it is, hiring a detective online carries risk if you don't carefully check the provider's credibility. To avoid being scammed or having your personal data leaked, keep these points in mind:

- **Choose a credible source.** Check that the website or platform you're using is real — read about the detective agency and look for clear contact details such as a phone number, address or contact form.
- **Read genuine reviews.** Reviews from previous clients are a key indicator of whether a provider can be trusted. Look at reviews on the website and on other platforms — Facebook, Google Reviews or Pantip — before deciding.
- **Agree the timeframe and scope.** Before hiring, discuss and agree clearly on what needs to be done, how long it will take, and any limits or conditions — to prevent misunderstandings and problems later.
- **Get clear pricing.** The cost should be stated clearly from the start — any upfront fee, extra charges for deeper information, and other fees. Transparent pricing reduces worry and disputes down the line.
