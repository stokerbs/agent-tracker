When love turns to conflict and betrayal, many people look for a way forward by filing an adultery claim. But such a claim relies on proper, complete evidence — and a detective is the expert in gathering it. In this article, we'll help you understand what to know before hiring a detective to collect evidence for an adultery case.

The first thing to know is that whatever your status with your partner — husband, wife, or married without registration — an adultery claim depends on evidence. Each case needs specific evidence to support the claim. Under the Civil and Commercial Code, the key evidence in an adultery case includes proof of suspicious conduct and behaviour, conversations or messages that serve as evidence, and objects used in the wrongdoing.

Gathering this evidence is a matter of timing. If you leave it too long, it may be lost or lose its value. So for anyone who sees — or suspects — that their partner is having an affair, the important thing is to act quickly to secure evidence that will matter to the case later.

One point worth noting: you can claim damages from the third party without getting divorced. Detectives are experienced in helping you find the evidence to support such a claim. In some cases, family happiness, love, or children make divorce impossible — and in those situations, an adultery claim may be what brings you some peace of mind.

In the end, choosing to hire a detective to gather adultery evidence takes careful thought. The information in this article should help you understand what to expect and what to know before taking the next step.
