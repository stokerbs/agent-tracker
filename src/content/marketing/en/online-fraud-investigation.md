In the digital age we live in, online fraud is a steadily growing problem. As technology advances, scammers keep inventing new ways to deceive people and take advantage of them financially and through their personal data. According to the US Federal Trade Commission (FTC), in 2022 consumers reported nearly $8.8 billion in fraud losses — up more than 30% on the previous year. The FBI, meanwhile, reported 800,944 cybercrime complaints in 2022; although that was down 5% from 2021, potential losses rose to $10.2 billion, from $6.9 billion the year before.

### New forms of online fraud

Online fraud takes many forms — phishing emails, fake websites, AI-generated scam messages, and even deepfake technology used to create fake video or audio. Each method is designed to lure users into revealing personal or financial information.

### How to protect yourself from online fraud

Protecting yourself takes awareness and keeping your knowledge current. Some guidelines:

- Be cautious of suspicious emails or messages.
- Use two-factor authentication.
- Keep your software and operating system updated to the latest version.
